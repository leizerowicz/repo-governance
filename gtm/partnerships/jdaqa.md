# JDAQA Partnership Notes

Jay Aigner · jdaqa.com · Philadelphia, PA · last reviewed 2026-05-20

## Who they are

JDAQA is Jay Aigner's firm. Positioned as **"Quality Infrastructure for AI-accelerated engineering teams."** 10+ years building quality systems; embedded with NeuroFlow, Penn Mutual, ABIM, and ArcWeb in Philly's healthtech and fintech ecosystem.

Recent rebrand (the 2026/05 image assets on the site suggest weeks-old) sharpened positioning around **AI quality debt**. They claim to be *"the only QA firm to have mapped Claude Code's specific quality failure modes: implementation bias, context pollution, visual blind spots, regression drift."*

Three-step engagement: Quality Debt Assessment → Build the System → Deploy. The Quality Debt Call is their free intake; the Assessment is the first paid deliverable. The Assessment scores debt across six dimensions: Financial, Product, Velocity, Infrastructure, Market, Security.

## Why the fit is unusually clean

- **Same buyer.** Both sell to CTO / VPE at AI-enabled mid-market companies.
- **Different layer.** Jay's product is diagnostic + roadmap + embedded execution. The repo-governance practice is one specific control system that operationalizes governance.
- **Direct mechanism overlap.** Jay named "regression drift" as one of four failure modes. The scheduled staleness audit is literally a mechanism for catching regression drift. Not coincidence — complementary.
- **Asymmetric strengths.** Jay has execution bandwidth (a firm), existing customer base, named-client credibility, and a sales motion. Greg has working methodology, real artifact (ai-fleet), and engineering-CTO credibility from Hopskip. Neither one of those is what the other has.
- **Geographic affinity.** Hainesport NJ ↔ Philly ≈ 20 min drive. In-person founding conversation is trivial logistically.

## Three partnership flavors (increasing commitment)

1. **Referral reciprocity.** Jay sends fractional-CTO / build-side asks to Greg; Greg sends quality-debt-flavored work to Jay. Low-effort, classic, works regardless of anything else.

2. **Sub-contract on "Build the System."** When Jay's Assessment surfaces governance gaps, Greg comes in (under Jay's banner or co-branded) to install the practice. Jay invoices the client; Greg invoices Jay. Jay gets a faster, more credible execution path than rebuilding governance bespoke per client.

3. **Methodology integration.** repo-governance becomes a named pattern in Jay's playbook — "JDAQA's governance overlay, built on the repo-governance framework." Co-marketing follows.

Don't try to negotiate (2) or (3) in the first conversation. (1) happens naturally if the meeting goes well; the others evolve from there.

## What to lead with

Honest opener (template — adapt to medium):

> *"You've been studying AI quality debt from the buyer side — 20 CTO conversations, four named failure modes. I've been building from the practitioner side — a governance practice that operationalizes regression drift detection, currently running in ai-fleet, written up at github.com/leizerowicz/repo-governance. I think what I have backs up what you've been hearing. Worth a coffee to see if there's something here?"*

Don't pitch a partnership. Propose exploration. The substance does the work.

## What to know going in

- He's actively building a narrative right now (recent rebrand). He's *receptive* to things that reinforce it but allergic to things that compete with or muddy it. Frame the practice as "one specific tool that operationalizes one of your four failure modes," not "an alternative to your assessment."
- Greg isn't yet a firm — one person with a methodology. Lean into it: "I'm not trying to compete with what you do; I'm building one piece that complements it."
- The "20 one-on-one CTO conversations" signal he's pre-sales-mode. He's heard the problems; he's looking for things that close the loop. That's the moment.
- **Don't lead with the BModelr/Jeff doc.** That's a tactical client review, not a positioning artifact. Lead with the repo-governance README and the principle ("enforcement ships with the promise, not after it"). The Jeff engagement becomes a case study after the fact, not a calling card upfront.
